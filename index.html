Cultural significance

The British and the English-speaking population of Ireland, Australia and Canada traditionally bake pancakes on "fat Tuesday" ("forgiven Sunday" before the beginning of Lent)[the source is not specified for 587 days]. On this day, many English housewives compete for the right to participate in the "pancake race" ‚Äî a 400-meter race among women carrying a hot pan with a pancake in their hands, which should be thrown at least twice on the run. The race starts at 11 a.m., when the church bells ring. The winner is the participant who manages to toss and flip the pancake in the pan the most times
